Erie Highlands June 2025

Overgrown corner lot back on track after a missed month

Add hero photo here

This stop came back onto the route after a month off. The grass had gone to nearly 8 inches in the back — thick enough that a single pass would have clumped and smothered what was underneath. Here's how we handled it and what the yard looked like by the time we left.

The situation

Corner lots grow fast because they catch more sun and often have two full street edges that need edging on both sides. When a yard like this goes uncut for a month in May and June — peak Colorado growing season — it compounds quickly.

What we did

  • First pass at high deck (4"). Knocking tall grass down in one pass risks clumping and stress on the root crowns. First pass removes the top third, bags the clippings.
  • Second pass at final height (3.5"). Crosscut direction for a cleaner finish and to stand up any grass that laid flat on the first pass.
  • Full perimeter edge. Both street sides, the driveway, and the front walk. Corner lots have a lot of linear feet — this took about 20 minutes on its own.
  • Flower bed cleanup. Debris and some encroaching grass along the bed edges cleaned up before blowing everything down.

The takeaway

A yard that gets away from you isn't ruined — it just takes more time to bring back. Two-pass mowing adds 15–20 minutes to the visit but it's the right call. Scalping overgrown turf in one pass stresses the grass and leaves clumps that block sun and cause brown spots.

If you're on a bi-weekly plan and your yard grows fast, it's worth talking about switching to weekly through peak season (May–July) and dropping back in August.
