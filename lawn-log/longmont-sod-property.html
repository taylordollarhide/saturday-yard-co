Longmont June 2025

11 acres south of Longmont — sod repair and fresh beds.

Not every job is a subdivision lot. This one is 11 acres south of Longmont — a working rural property with a beautiful old farmhouse and mature trees. The homeowners had some bare spots from winter damage and beds that needed fresh mulch. They wanted it looking sharp heading into summer.

Sod repair in the island beds

The center island beds had patches where the turf had died off along the edges — common where foot traffic and winter damage combine. We pulled the dead material, laid fresh sod to fill the gaps, and cleaned up the bed edges so the transitions were sharp going forward.

Mid-job — beds mulched

Finished

Fresh mulch in the beds

The island beds got a full refresh — old debris cleared out, fresh dark mulch laid around the shrubs. On a property this size, the beds anchor the lawn visually. Clean mulch makes the green pop and gives the whole yard a finished look even between mow cycles.

The result

Fresh sod knitting into the existing turf, clean bed lines, and dark mulch throughout. On a property this size the beds are what anchor everything visually — when they're tight and fresh, the whole place reads as maintained even between service visits.

The finished result is what this kind of property deserves — tight beds and fresh sod knitting into the existing turf. It photographs well because it was done right.
