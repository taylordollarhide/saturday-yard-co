Colliers Hill May 2025

New sod install — first season mowing tips

Add hero photo here

New sod looks great on day one. The risk is the first few mows — done wrong, you can pull up seams, stress shallow roots, and set the lawn back months. This Colliers Hill yard had sod laid in late April. Here's how we approached the first cuts of the season.

Wait longer than you think

Most people want to mow new sod as soon as it greens up. The rule is: wait until you can't easily pull up a corner. Tug on a seam — if it lifts with any resistance, wait another few days. Roots need to anchor before a mower deck passes over.

For this yard, the sod went down April 22nd. We held off until May 10th — nearly three weeks — before the first cut.

How we mowed it differently

  • Deck height at 4". Higher than normal. New sod shouldn't be cut below 3.5" for the first two months — the root system can't handle the stress of low mowing yet.
  • Slow, straight passes. No tight turns on new sod. We made wide, gradual turns at the end of each row to avoid torquing the turf.
  • No edging along the seams. The edges of new sod are the most vulnerable. We kept the trimmer well away from seam lines for the first month.
  • Left the clippings. Short clippings at 4" break down fast and return moisture to the soil — important while roots are still establishing.

What to watch for

Seam lines that stay visible after 3–4 weeks usually mean inconsistent watering. The gaps between sod pieces dry out faster than the centers. If you see brown lines following the seam pattern, bump up your watering frequency — not duration — until they fill in.

By mid-June this yard had fully knit together and we dropped back to our normal 3.5" deck height. It's tracking well.
